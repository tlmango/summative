Environmental Impact of Computer Usage

Made by MING

In this website, we'll be talking about both the good and the bad of computer usage.

What are the environmental consequences of computer device production?

All computer problems relate to environmental pollution and health issues. Improper disposal can spread diseases, so proper disposal is important. Computer parts are valuable; recycling these parts can reduce environmental impact. Instead of throwing your computer out, recycle it to minimize landfill waste, hazardous materials, e-waste, and greenhouse gas emissions.

  • Main computer impacts:
  • Energy consumption
  • Electronic waste
  • Manufacturing process waste

Heavy Metal Contamination: Computers contain heavy metals such as lead, mercury, and cadmium. When discarded in landfills, these metals can leach into the ground, contaminating soil and groundwater.

How does computer use harm the environment?

Using a screen for 8 years results in greenhouse gas emissions equivalent to 70g CO2e from energy consumption.

Common setups and their carbon footprints include:

  • Desktop + screen: 621kg CO2e
  • Laptop + screen: 691kg CO2e (+11%)
  • Desktop + 2 screens: 903kg CO2e (+45%)
  • Laptop + screen at office + screen at home: 928kg CO2e (+49%)
  • Desktop + screen + laptop: 1,030kg CO2e (+66%)

The average desktop consumes 40W of electricity, which contributes to CO2 emissions.
